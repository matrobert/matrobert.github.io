Undergraduate Research
-----------------------

My previous research focused on studying glioblastoma pathogenesis from an epigenetic standpoint. Using glioma stem cells, I seek to understand the intrinsic mechanisms of abberant epigenetic regulators that may contribute to glioblastoma tumorigenesis and pathogenic properties. This project has culminated to my bachelor's thesis. 

Prior to glioblastoma research, I was also involved in another project on plant development where I focused on elucidating potential signaling cross-talk in stomatal development and plant disease resistance pathway. I utilized *Arabidopsis* loss-of-function mutants to investigate plant disease resistance genes that may modulate stomatal number & maturation, thereby potentially acting as bridge that integrates plant defense response and stomatal development.
